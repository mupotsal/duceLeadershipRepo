Charline Prazen Chikomo A.K.A DUCE OF AFRICA was born in Mberengwa ( Zimbabwe on 30 January 1999. The life story of Charline Prazen Chikomo ( Duce of Africa) can never be explained exclusively from dropping out of school, homelessness, hunger, and starvation failing to afford education, or uniform to wear, hit by the reality of life, abandonment, and the pain of poverty, going to school with only three things with him: oversized trousers that will become a symbol of identification for a long time, a cream shirt rather than a white one that was required, and a big dream in his head that scares him every night. For many at that time, his look was a sight of laughter, just as bullying, rejection, and isolation became things that he would have to confront day by day. But with ideas in his head, a dream insight, and a purpose to drive him, he persevered through all that life had to offer him.

Out of that laughter and development, isolation and bullying came a mountain of strength, courage, and motivation. Due to financial and family-related issues, Charline Chikomo had to miss school from 2005- 2010, which was a challenging time for him associated with all the problems mentioned above. Through the help of good wishers and extended family, Charline Chikomo attended grade 7 at Shenjere Primary School in Gutu. It was a difficult moment for him because he has not been going to school all this time. However, he managed to come up with 14 Units but very promising because he is someone who has been through a lot during such a young age. He was fortunate enough to start his form 1 in Masvingo in January 2012, making his first appearance in an urban area through the help of good wishers whom among them include but not limited to the teachers of Ndarama High School who from his story saw great potential and decided to help. In 2014, however, he made a turnaround decision that his background is not the reason for me to be at the back or an excuse for failure but as the chief reason why he should be more severe than anyone else. He was in the top ten, got 7As and Bs at an ordinary level, and finally 20points Advanced level ( History A, Shona A, Divinity A, and Geography A ). He also pushed me to find myself in the Junior Parliament of Zimbabwe, pushing the agenda of the young people. It was during his time at High School and with his strong desire to help other that he was appointed to save in the Junior Parliament of Zimbabwe advocating for Children's rights, he was the Vice Headboy, Chairman of the Scripture Union, Public Speaking and Debate, Chairman of the Environmental Club, but also engaged in charitable works that included helping children living in the streets to read and write, fundraising for the orphanages and the Old Peoples homes. To mention but few. As one of the national best students, he found that as an opportunity to start solving that problem that affected him for so many years, he dropped out of school. He registered his youth-led organization, which was called" Duce Tutoring," that works on educating those who can not afford through seminars and fundraising to raise their examination fees. The founding ideal of Duce Tutoring is anchored in the very belief that made the Teachers of Ndarama feed him, clothe him, and give him hope; believe that each one of us is because the rest are. For him, there could be no genuine way of paying back then committed to educating the next generation and provide a way out for those who have the courage, have the brilliance,

and dream the ambition of doing great things in life but do not have the means to do so.He also managed to write books in trying to solve the problem of shortage of textbooks in rural areas, for example, at Shenjere Primary school, where they used to read one book, 40 students, once making it difficult to pass. Seminars were also done all over the country by the 18-year-Old - Charline Prazen Chikomo. Not forgetting the fact that inspirational sessions were also done with him using his story as a practical example that it is doable no matter the background. In his words, he would always say, "Loss of a battle does not signal the end of the war, keep your guns blazing until you win the war for failure is not failure but a step towards success." What was surprising is not just that he was 18 years old but also the fact that he did have any donors or leaders working with him, but he desired to educate them so that they could turn out to be leaders and donors in the organization. That's when he adopted the theme, "Educate them to educate others. Many students performed so well from these tutorials, but after these high school lessons, many would be found in the streets without jobs.
